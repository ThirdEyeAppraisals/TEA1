Insurance Dispute Resolution

The Appraisal Clause

When your insurance company offers a settlement that does not reflect the true value of your vehicle, your policy includes an Appraisal Clause — a provision that allows you to demand an independent valuation.

This is your right as a policyholder. Third Eye Appraisals serves as your independent appraiser, fighting for a fair and accurate vehicle valuation.

Dispute a Low Settlement Offer?

Do not accept a payout that undervalues your vehicle. Invoke your appraisal clause and let us help.

Request Appraisal
1

Review Your Policy

Most auto insurance policies include an appraisal clause. Check your policy documents or ask your insurer to confirm it exists.

2

Invoke the Clause in Writing

Submit a written demand for appraisal to your insurance company. This triggers the formal appraisal process.

3

Hire Your Independent Appraiser

Select Third Eye Appraisals as your independent appraiser. We conduct a thorough inspection and determine the true market value of your vehicle.

4

Negotiation & Resolution

Our valuation is compared with the insurance company's appraiser. If the two appraisers agree, the value is set. If not, a neutral umpire makes the final determination.
